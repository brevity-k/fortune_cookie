On March 20, 2025 at 09:01 UTC, the Sun crosses into 0 degrees Aries, and the astrological year resets. If you've been waiting for permission to start over, this is it — the cosmos is handing you a clean slate at 4:01 a.m. Pacific, 7:01 a.m. Eastern, just before most of the United States finishes its first cup of coffee.

This is the vernal equinox, the moment when day and night stand at roughly 12 hours each across the globe, and when tropical astrology marks its true new year. Forget January 1. Aries season is when the soil softens, the light tips toward us, and your nervous system finally exhales. The one-line takeaway: this is the week to plant the seed of one specific thing you want to grow by autumn — not five things, one — and to act on it before the Sun leaves the first degree of Aries on March 21.

## What's actually happening up there

The word equinox comes from Latin — aequus meaning equal, nox meaning night. Twice a year, the Sun crosses the celestial equator, the imaginary line projected outward from Earth's equator into space. On March 20, it crosses moving northward, and that exact crossing point is called the First Point of Aries. In tropical astrology, this is degree zero of the zodiac — the cardinal ignition switch.

Aries is a cardinal fire sign, ruled by Mars. Cardinal means initiating; fire means action without a permission slip. When the Sun ingresses into Aries, the entire chart of the moment becomes the chart of the astrological year ahead — what astrologers call the Aries Ingress chart, used since at least the Hellenistic period to forecast the tone of the next twelve months. Contemporary astrologers like Chani Nicholas, Austin Coppock, and the late Robert Hand have all written about reading the equinox chart as a kind of cosmic weather report.

Here's the historical thread worth pulling. At Chichen Itza on the Yucatán Peninsula, the El Castillo pyramid — also called the Temple of Kukulkan — was engineered so that at the spring equinox, the afternoon sun casts a triangular shadow pattern down the northern staircase that looks unmistakably like a serpent descending. Thousands of people show up every March to watch it happen. At Stonehenge in Wiltshire, England, the sunrise on the equinox aligns with specific stones in ways that 5,000-year-old hands clearly intended. These weren't decorations. They were calendars, and the people who built them organized planting, harvesting, marriages, and royal ceremonies around this exact moment.

Then there's Nowruz, the Persian New Year, celebrated at the spring equinox for more than 3,000 years from Iran through Tajikistan, Afghanistan, and Kurdish communities worldwide. Families set a haft-sin table with seven symbolic items, jump over small bonfires on the Wednesday before to burn off the previous year's misfortune, and eat sabzi polo with fish. The 2024 equinox arrived on March 19 at 03:06 UTC; this year it lands 30 hours later and slightly differently configured. That six-hour annual drift is exactly why leap years exist — to keep the equinox roughly anchored to the same calendar weekend.

The last time the Sun-Moon configuration at the equinox emphasized communication and restless thinking (as the 2025 chart does, with Mercury retrograde in Aries from March 15 through April 7) was 2013. Think back to spring 2013 if you can — what conversations did you have that changed your direction? Themes tend to echo.

## What this means for the actual life you are living

In love and partnership, Aries season favors directness. If you've been drafting a text in your head for three weeks, send it. The Mercury retrograde overlay means old flames and unfinished conversations from the past two years may reappear, especially between March 20 and April 7. Don't panic if your ex shows up in your DMs — but don't sign a lease together either. Use the contact for closure, not commitment. For couples, this is a strong window for honest, slightly uncomfortable conversations about what each of you actually wants from the year ahead. If you want to look ahead to specific themes, [love fortunes](/fortune/love) can give you a prompt to chew on.

At work, the energy is forward-thrusting but the retrograde adds caution. Launch the project you've been planning, but hold off on signing major contracts or making irreversible hires until after April 7. This is an excellent two-week window to pitch ideas, reach out to mentors, ask for the raise, or begin researching a career pivot. The chart specifically favors people in creative, athletic, entrepreneurial, and first-responder fields — anyone whose work involves initiating something where nothing was before.

In your body, Aries rules the head, face, and adrenal system. Many people feel a surge of restless energy around the equinox that, if not channeled physically, turns into headaches, jaw tension, and 3 a.m. anxiety. The fix is almost embarrassingly simple: move. A 25-minute walk at sunrise does more for an Aries-season nervous system than any supplement. If you're an Aries sun, moon, or rising, expect this entire month to feel like someone replaced your morning coffee with rocket fuel. Lean into it, but sleep when you're tired.

In money, the equinox is a traditional moment for setting intentions about resources. Persian Nowruz traditions include placing a coin on the haft-sin table to invite prosperity. You don't need a haft-sin table. You need to look honestly at one financial number you've been avoiding — credit card balance, retirement contribution, monthly subscription total — and write it down.

## Six rituals for the equinox window

**1. Set your intention at 09:01 UTC on March 20 (or the closest hour you're awake).** Write one sentence, by hand, naming the single most important thing you want to begin this year. Not five things. One. Aries energy splinters when overloaded. Keep the paper somewhere you'll see it through April.

**2. Clean one specific space before March 20.** Nowruz tradition calls this khaneh tekani — literally "shaking the house." Pick your desk, your car, your nightstand, or the inside of your refrigerator. Not the whole house. One space, cleared completely, scrubbed, and reset by the morning of the equinox. The psychological reset is real and immediate.

**3. Light a red or orange candle on the evening of March 20.** Aries' colors are flame-toned, and fire is its element. Let it burn while you eat dinner. Watch the flame for two full minutes without your phone. This sounds small. It is not small — it's a 120-second nervous system recalibration that ancient cultures built entire temples around.

**4. Have the conversation you've been avoiding between March 20 and March 28.** Mercury retrograde in Aries makes this both harder and more necessary. Pick the person, pick the topic, and book the call or coffee. The conversation does not need to resolve anything — it needs to begin.

**5. Walk outside at sunrise at least three times this week.** The equinox is the one week of the year when sunrise and sunset are almost mathematically balanced. Your circadian rhythm uses this signal to recalibrate the entire next quarter. If sunrise is too early, walk within 30 minutes of waking — the principle holds.

**6. Pull a fortune or a card on the morning of March 21.** The day after the ingress, when the Sun sits at 1° Aries, is traditionally used for divination about the year ahead. [Break a fortune cookie](/), draw a tarot card, or check [your horoscope](/horoscope) — whatever practice you trust. Write down what you receive and revisit it at the autumn equinox in September. You will be startled by what shows up.

**7. Eat something green and freshly sprouted.** Pea shoots, sprouted lentils, microgreens, fresh parsley. The Nowruz tradition of sabzeh — growing wheat or lentil sprouts in a dish on the table — exists because eating what has just emerged from the soil is a way of telling your body that winter is over. Your nervous system is older than your calendar, and it listens.

**8. Name one thing you're letting end.** New beginnings require completed endings. Before March 21, name one habit, one grudge, one assumption, or one identity you carried through winter that you are not bringing into spring. Say it out loud, even if you're alone in your kitchen.

## A grounding note

The cosmos is real, but so is your need to rest. The equinox is not a deadline. If you sleep through March 20 at 09:01 UTC, the Sun will still be in Aries when you wake up, and the new year will still be yours. The ancient builders at Stonehenge and Chichen Itza weren't asking their communities to perform — they were marking a moment that the body already knows. Light a candle when you can. Walk when you can. Begin the thing, even badly. That's the whole instruction. Spring is patient, and so, mostly, are you.
